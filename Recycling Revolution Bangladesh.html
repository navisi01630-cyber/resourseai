Bangladesh Recycling Report

Current challenges, progress, and opportunities

Transforming Waste into Resources

Bangladesh generates over 25,000 tonnes of solid waste daily, with urban areas facing the most severe challenges. Only 45-50% of urban waste is formally collected, leading to environmental pollution and public health risks.

The recycling industry employs over 100,000 informal workers, including children, who work without safety gear or fair wages. Plastic waste has tripled in Dhaka since 2010, reaching 646 tonnes per day.

Despite these challenges, Bangladesh is making progress with initiatives like the National 3R Strategy, Solid Waste Management Rules 2021, and the National Action Plan for Sustainable Plastic Management which targets 50% recycling by 2025.

Scaling recycling could supply 75% of factory plastic needs, earning Tk 7 billion ($67M) annually in foreign exchange. New EU regulations will create export opportunities for recycled materials.
